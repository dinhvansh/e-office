# Trademark Policy

This draft explains how the E-Office name, logos, product names, and related
marks may be referenced. It does not grant trademark ownership or broad brand
rights.

## General rule

The Community Source License does not grant trademark, logo, trade dress,
domain name, product naming, or brand rights. You may not use E-Office marks in
a way that suggests endorsement, sponsorship, affiliation, certification, or
origin of a derivative product or hosted service without written permission.

## Allowed nominative references

Without separate written permission, you may truthfully refer to E-Office to:

- identify that your deployment, integration, plugin, training, consulting, or
  support service relates to E-Office;
- describe compatibility with E-Office;
- credit the upstream project in a fork or internal deployment;
- link to the upstream project or license materials.

These references must be accurate, limited, and not more prominent than your
own name or brand.

## Restricted uses

You need written permission before you:

- use E-Office logos or stylized marks in a product, service, marketing site,
  marketplace listing, or customer proposal;
- name a hosted, managed, white-label, OEM, or redistributed product in a way
  that implies it is an official E-Office offering;
- register domains, social accounts, app listings, package names, or company
  names containing E-Office or confusingly similar marks;
- remove E-Office attribution while presenting a derivative as an official
  E-Office product;
- use E-Office marks in paid advertising in a way that may confuse customers.

## Forks and modifications

Forks, internal modifications, and derivative works must not imply that they
are official E-Office releases unless the maintainer has approved that
representation in writing. Modified versions should clearly identify that they
are modified.

## Commercial licenses

A Commercial License does not automatically include trademark rights. Any
trademark grant must be explicit in the commercial agreement or another written
permission.
