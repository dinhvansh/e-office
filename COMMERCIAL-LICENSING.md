# Commercial Licensing

FlowDocker E-Office is published under a source-available Community Source
License. The
community license allows internal self-hosting, internal modification,
evaluation, learning, non-commercial personal use, development/testing, and
contribution. It does not grant commercial resale, hosted resale, white-label,
OEM, embedded distribution, or competing redistribution rights.

This document explains when a separate commercial license is expected. It is an
informational draft for maintainer review and does not itself grant additional
rights or create an offer.

## Definitions

- **Internal Business Use**: using FlowDocker E-Office for your own organization,
  employees, contractors, internal users, and internal workflows.
- **Commercial Hosting**: operating FlowDocker E-Office, or a service substantially based
  on FlowDocker E-Office, for third-party paid or monetized access.
- **Managed Service**: running or administering FlowDocker E-Office for a third party in a
  way that makes FlowDocker E-Office the product or a substantial part of the product
  delivered to that third party.
- **White-Label**: presenting FlowDocker E-Office or a service substantially based on it
  under another brand, product name, logo, or domain in a way that hides or
  replaces the FlowDocker E-Office identity.
- **OEM / Embedded Distribution**: bundling, embedding, or shipping FlowDocker E-Office
  inside another product, appliance, platform, package, or service distributed
  to third parties.
- **Redistribution**: making modified or unmodified copies of FlowDocker E-Office
  available to third parties through source code, binaries, containers,
  packages, marketplaces, downloads, or similar channels.
- **Commercial License**: a separate written agreement with the copyright
  holder or authorized maintainer that grants rights beyond the Community
  Source License.

## Allowed without a Commercial License

You may use the Community Source License for:

- internal self-hosting for your own organization;
- internal modifications for your own use;
- evaluation, learning, demos, development, and testing;
- non-commercial personal use;
- submitting patches, issues, documentation, and other contributions;
- paid consulting, implementation, training, migration, integration, or support
  around FlowDocker E-Office, provided the service does not effectively resell
  FlowDocker E-Office
  itself as hosted access, a managed service, a white-label product, OEM
  distribution, or commercial redistribution.

## Requires a Commercial License

Contact the maintainers before:

- selling hosted access to FlowDocker E-Office;
- offering FlowDocker E-Office as SaaS or a service bureau;
- providing a managed hosting service where the substantial value is FlowDocker E-Office;
- white-labeling or rebranding FlowDocker E-Office for customers;
- embedding FlowDocker E-Office in another commercial product or platform;
- distributing modified or unmodified copies commercially as a competing
  product;
- sublicensing FlowDocker E-Office or granting broader downstream rights.

## Trademark and branding

Commercial licensing does not automatically grant trademark or logo rights.
Brand use requires written permission unless TRADEMARK.md expressly permits the
specific nominative reference.
